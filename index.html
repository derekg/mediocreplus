Here's the truth: you don't need to be great. You just need to be slightly less dumb than the room.

The average is lower than you think. The bar is ankle height and most people still trip over it. Read one book and you're "thoughtful." Do the math and you're "strategic." Follow through and you're "elite." The gap between average and impressive is about 90 minutes of real effort.

Most people don't prepare. They don't think from first principles. They don't check assumptions. They don't finish what they start. They attend meetings like spectators at their own careers.

If you show up awake, you're already dangerous.

Here's the arbitrage: competence is rare. Clarity is rarer. Consistency is nearly extinct. Be Mediocre Plus and you print reputation.

You don't need to be 10x. Most so-called exceptional leaders are 1.1x operating in rooms that are 0.7x. That small delta becomes mythology. Decisiveness looks like courage. Basic math looks like strategy. Calm looks like wisdom. Saying no looks like strength.

+0.5 in a room of −0.5 is "visionary."

Stop trying to be legendary. Be slightly sharper. Slightly faster. Slightly clearer. Do it consistently.

In a world sliding backward, Mediocre Plus looks unstoppable.

That's the cheat code.
